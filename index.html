IV. Subjective Questions

1. Derive Voltage Gain for Single Tuned Amplifier.

The gain is calculated by dividing the output voltage by the input voltage. At resonance, the reactance of L and C cancel out, leaving only the resistance. Gain = (gm times Load Resistance).

2. Why is Class A efficiency limited to 25%?

In series-fed Class A, the DC bias current flows even without a signal. This means the transistor wastes most power as heat. The AC output power is only one-fourth of the total DC input power.

3. Explain Class B Push-Pull operation.

Two transistors are used. One handles the positive half-cycle, the other handles the negative. This is why it is called 'push-pull'. It uses a center-tapped transformer for phase splitting.

4. Derive Resonance Frequency for parallel tuned circuit.

At resonance, Inductive reactance equals Capacitive reactance. By setting (2 * pi * f * L) equal to (1 divided by 2 * pi * f * C), we find f = 1 divided by (2 * pi * square root of LC).

5. Operation of Single Tuned Capacitively Coupled Amplifier.

The input is coupled via a capacitor. The load consists of a parallel LC tank circuit that resonates at the desired frequency, providing maximum gain only at that frequency.

6. Find reflected load and AC power (10:1 transformer, 8 ohm load, 20V VCC).

Reflected Load = Load Resistance times (Turns Ratio squared). Here, 8 ohms times (10 squared) = 800 ohms. AC power is calculated using the peak voltage swing.

7. Double Tuned Amplifier and Coupling Coefficient k.

It uses two LC circuits. If k is small, the curve is narrow. If k is critical, gain is max. If k is larger than critical, the curve splits into two peaks, increasing bandwidth.
