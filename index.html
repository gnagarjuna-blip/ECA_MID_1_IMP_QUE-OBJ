IV. Subjective Questions

1. Explain why Series-fed Class A efficiency is limited to 25%.

In series-fed Class A, the DC bias current flows through the load at all times, even when no signal is present. This results in massive power wastage as heat in the transistor. Theoretical maximum AC power is only 1/4 of the DC power supplied.

2. Describe the working of a Class B Push-Pull amplifier.

It uses two transistors: one for the positive half-cycle and one for the negative half-cycle. A center-tapped transformer provides the necessary phase splitting so that each transistor conducts for exactly 180 degrees.

3. What is the effect of coupling coefficient (k) in Double Tuned Amplifiers?

- Under-critical coupling: Low gain, narrow peak.
- Critical coupling: Maximum gain and optimum bandwidth.
- Over-critical coupling: The gain curve develops a 'double hump' and the bandwidth increases significantly.
